## 💖 Featured Projects

<details>
<summary><b>🏥 Healthcare & SDoH Analysis — 7.6M encounters, 339K patients · 🥇 DataFest 2026 Best Use of External Data</b></summary>

<br>

Investigated how social determinants of health (SDoH) shape chronic care utilization, using Stormont Vail Health records enriched with U.S. Census TIGER/ACS block-group data.

| Category | Details |
|---------|--------|
| Stack | R (ggplot2), Python, Pandas |
| Scale | 7.6M encounters · 339K patients · 1.37 GB raw data across six CSVs |
| Cohort | 68,729 patients with chronic conditions (Dorsalgia, Type 2 Diabetes, Obesity), incl. 1,464 with all three |
| Headline Finding | Patients with transportation barriers attended **43% more visits** and used Emergency Medicine **~2× as often** (ANOVA F = 276.6, p < 0.001) |
| Rural Access Gap | Osage City: 210 T2D patients per relevant provider vs. 82 in Topeka |
| Output | 20+ publication-quality visualizations, two consolidated master datasets, operational recommendations |

Built a reproducible R pipeline that cleaned and joined six raw datasets, engineered SDoH flags across five domains (Transportation, Financial, Food, Utilities, Housing), and translated findings into a concrete recommendation: pair transportation assistance with chronic disease management to reduce avoidable ER use.

</details>
